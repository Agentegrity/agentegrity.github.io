The agentic risk surface

Agents act. Who guarantees they act with integrity?

A single compromised instruction can cascade across tools, data, and downstream agents. Traditional controls were built for code and users — not for autonomous systems that reason and decide.

Prompt injection

Untrusted content hijacks an agent's intent, turning a helpful assistant into an attacker's proxy.

Tool misuse

Over-privileged agents invoke the wrong action, exfiltrate data, or move money without a verifiable check.

Cascading compromise

One flawed agent poisons the next. Multi-agent chains amplify a small failure into systemic risk.

The platform

An integrity layer for the entire agent lifecycle.

From build-time policy to runtime enforcement to tamper-evident audit — Agentegrity wraps every agent and tool call in continuous, verifiable trust.

Runtime attestation

Cryptographically prove what an agent is, what it's allowed to do, and that it did exactly that — on every decision.

Behavioral guardrails

Detect and block prompt injection, jailbreaks, and intent drift before a single tool is touched.

Policy enforcement

Declarative, least-privilege policy that validates every tool invocation against approved actions in real time.

Agent identity

Strong, verifiable identity for every agent and sub-agent, so you always know who is acting and on whose authority.

Tamper-evident audit

An append-only, signed record of every prompt, decision, and action — provable to auditors and regulators.

Live observability

Stream integrity signals into your SIEM and dashboards. Catch deviation from baseline the moment it happens.

FAQ

Questions, answered.

What exactly does Agentegrity protect?

Any system where an AI agent reasons and then acts — calling tools, APIs, databases, or other agents. Agentegrity governs the decision-to-action boundary where the real risk lives.

Do I have to rebuild my agents?

No. Integrate via the SDK or run agents behind the Agentegrity gateway. Your orchestration, models, and tools stay exactly as they are.

Which models and frameworks are supported?

Agentegrity is model- and framework-agnostic. If your agent makes tool calls, it can be attested, governed, and audited.
